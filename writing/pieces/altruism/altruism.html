One positive trend that started on social media was people asking for donations to a charity of choice for their birthday*. I do truly feel that the best way you can give to me is to donate one of the initatives that I care about:

(1) Donate to IOCDF Annual Fund (OCD initiative), Hope for the Day (mental health and suicide prevention initative), Brain & Behavior Research Foundation (research to find cures for mental illness).
(2) Donate to SPCA of Solano County (amazing animal shelter that doesn't euthanize), ASPCA (more general Society for the Prevention of Cruelty to Animals).
(3) Donate to Innocence Project (exonerate the wrongfully convicted), Prisoners Literature Project (provides free books for inmates).
(4) Donate to Anima International (effective altruism for factory farming)^.
(5) Donate to Syrian American Medical Foundation (medical relief (including mental health) on frontlines in Syria), Palestine Children's Relief Fund (treat difficult cases and train Palestinian surgeons).
(6) Donate to GiveDirectly (cash transfers for extreme poverty), Action Against Hunger (feed the hungry and more), National Alinace to End Homelessness (help communities implement solutions to end homelesness).

If there's a charity that does good with their money and encompasses these themes, please direct me to it by emailing dannyhalawi15@gmail.com

If you resonate with the more canonical effective altruism see givewell.

If you don't have the means to donate, one idea that Brian suggested (and did) was to make meals and give them out to homeless people; if money is really tight, maybe you can give your leftovers from your own meals; and if you cannot do that, then "your smile for your brother is charity."

*There are 2.91 billion active facebook users. Say 5% engage in this trend: 2.91 billion x .05 = 145,500,000. Say each one raise on average 50 dollars for their birthday: 145,500,000 x $50 = seven billion two hundred seventy-five million dollars. On October 28 (2021), David Beasley, director of the UN's World Food Programme, tweeted that if Elon Musk donated 6 billion dollars, it would save 42 million lives that are "knocking on famine's door". Therefore, this trend is far from trivial... sevon billion can do a lot of good, which can't be overstated.
