Altruism

One positive trend that started on social media was people asking for donations to a charity of choice for their birthday1. I do truly feel that the best way you can give to me is to donate one of the initatives that I care about:

(1) Donate to IOCDF Annual Fund (OCD and related disorders initiative), Hope for the Day (mental health and suicide prevention initative), Brain & Behavior Research Foundation (research to find cures for mental illness).
(2) Donate to SPCA of Solano County (amazing animal shelter that doesn't euthanize), ASPCA (more general Society for the Prevention of Cruelty to Animals).
(3) Donate to Innocence Project (exonerate the wrongfully convicted), Prisoners Literature Project (provides free books for inmates).
(4) Donate to Anima International (effective altruism for factory farming)2.
(5) Donate to Syrian American Medical Foundation (medical relief (including mental health) on frontlines in Syria), Palestine Children's Relief Fund (treats injured/sick children and train Palestinian surgeons).
(6) Donate to GiveDirectly (cash transfers for extreme poverty), Action Against Hunger (feeds the hungry and more), National Alinace to End Homelessness (helps communities implement solutions to end homelesness).

If there's a charity that does good with their money and encompasses these themes, please direct me to it by emailing dannyhalawi15 (at) gmail (dot) com.

If you resonate with the more canonical effective altruism see givewell and see the piece I wrote on effective altruism and giving.

If you don't have the means to donate, one idea that an acquaintence suggested (and did) was to make meals and give them out to homeless people; if money is really tight, maybe you can give your leftovers from your own meals; and if you cannot do that, then "your smile for your brother is charity."
